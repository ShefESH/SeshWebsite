We are SESH - University of Sheffield's Ethical Student Hackers Society

We deliver regular sessions covering a variety of cybersecurity topics

We'll teach you all the theory, including web application attacks and buffer overflows.

And we'll go from the theory to applying it to ethical hacking!

To teach you this we do live demos, CTF challenges, theory lectures, worksheets and more!

We formed in 2018, and for the 2021-22 academic year we want to keep expanding our beginner resources by releasing our Fundamental Skills series and continuing to create regular practice material.

We're also always trying to expand our links with industry, and have some fun guest talks planned this year!

Scroll down to find out more about our society, or jump to our list of upcoming events or our plan for the year.

Who We Are

We have a diverse society and anyone from the University of Sheffield is welcome! You can be confident that members of any ability are welcome to join - we want to provide something for everyone, no matter your experience.

For non-University of Sheffield students, you can still access a number of free resources on our wiki, and potentially even participate in joint events or CTFs in the future.

All of our committee have a keen interest Cybersecurity, and a wide range of experiences and knowledge that we're excited to share with you - from web design to hardware! Meet our committee here!

Anyone is welcome to join committee, regardless of their background - if you want to join, contact us!

What You'll Learn

Cybersecurity Fundamentals

From the basics of Linux to the nitty gritty of networking, we'll teach you everything you need to know to get started in Cybersecurity.

We know how daunting it can be to start off in such a complicated field - that's why this year we're releasing a series of Fundamental Skills mini-lectures that you can optionally use to consolidate your knowledge before coming to a session, or just to learn a new skill.

Cybersecurity Tools

We'll take you through how to use some of the most common tools in Cybersecurity - nmap, Wireshark, Burp Suite, and more!

We'll also teach you how to build your own tools, using common languages like Python for automating tasks, enumerating websites and networks, and even cracking passwords.

Ethical Hacking

Perhaps the most exciting and varied area of Cybersecurity, ethical hacking (or penetration testing) is a challenging, fun and rewarding way to test your skills - and it can lead to a lucrative career!

We'll teach you the tools and techniques you'll need, the theory behind what you're doing, and methodologies such as enumeration and privilege escalation.

Then we'll provide a platform to practice your new skills with challenges, Capture the Flag events, and walkthroughs of boxes on platforms like Hack The Box, VulnHub, and TryHackMe.

Plan for the Year

Accessibility

As we come back to in-person events, we want to continue making our sessions as accessible as possible for all of our members.

That's why we plan to continue recording and livestreaming our sessions on Blackboard Collaborate - this means if you can't make a session one week, or don't feel comfortable attending in person, you won't have to miss out.

We're also going to make our sessions shorter after some feedback from last year, and will be providing a short break in the middle of sessions.

Remember that you can contact our Inclusions Officer Seb if you have any concerns about accessibility.

Balancing Theory & Practical

Our aim last year was to introduce more hands-on elements into our sessions in the form of worksheets. These were released weekly at the start of the year, but we struggled to create them for every session.

This year, to make practical elements more consistent, we're going to split sessions roughly 50/50 in terms of theory and practical! We'll include worksheets or follow-along demos as part of every session, with a mix of easy and hard material.

Beginner Resources

As with last year, we'll begin the first semester by teaching the fundamental stages of a penetration test, and exploring common web application attacks and the basics of Command Line-based operating systems.

This should give you a good baseline as we work up to a practice penetration test at the end of the semester!

We're also going to release our Fundamental Skills series, which looks to create a list of key skills that everyone in Cyber should understand; we'll cover these in sessions too, but the series will take a deeper look at them so you can get ahead or have a recap.

While we want to create resources for beginners, our returning members are just as important to us - we're going to mix up the practice platforms this year so you won't be repeating what you did last year, and introduce some cool new topics.

Events and Collaborations

We want to continue organising exciting guest talks, society collaborations, and CTF Events this year.

Our BakeryTF event was one of the highlights of last academic year - we want to make a bigger and better CTF this time, and host it earlier in the year to avoid the busiest exam periods!
